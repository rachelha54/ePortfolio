Undergraduate Researcher

@Plasma & Dielectrics Lab

Jan 2024 -  Feb 2025

Dr. Lukas Graber

Georgia Tech School of Electrical and Computer Engineering

My Experience:

I joined the PDL after being fascinated by Lukas's presentation in a course I was taking. While there were many projects he had going on, I was excited to join the High-Voltage Bushing Design, Fabrication, and Automation project. I worked in the lab full-time over the summer and my tasks ranged from phone calls to manufacturers to finite element analysis of various bushing properties. As it was my first "work" experience, it was nerve-wracking, but also exciting and new to be working on such a large-scale project. Through it, I also learned a lot about myself as a researcher and growing engineer. Overall, my time in the PDL was an incredible learning experience, allowing me to further appreciate materials science, automation, and the entire process in between. I am truly grateful to Lukas and everyone else in the PDL for such a terrific experience.

 

My Contributions:

- Evaluated and selected thermoset materials, liaised with manufacturers, and presented findings, leading to the adoption of optimized bushing materials enhancing tensile strength, thermal conductivity, and specific heat capacity

- Automated and streamlined the bushing fabrication process using LabVIEW, accounting for precision and safety in pressurized systems for thermoset quantities ranging from 10g to 125kg

 

Skills I Learned:

- NI LabVIEW

- COMSOL Multiphysics

- Comparing materials, liaising with manufacturers, and identifying optimal thermosets

- Developing state and schematic diagrams for the bushing fabrication process

- Automating components within the schematic one-by-one in NI LabVIEW

Student Researcher

July 2021 -  June 2022

UCONN School of Computer Science and Engineering

My Experience:

In high school, I took a class called Advanced Research Mentorship in which we worked alongside a professor for a year-long project. Having previous enjoyed AP CSA, I decided I wanted to further explore the realm of computer science. I worked with Derek at UCONN who advised me each week on how to grow as a programmer. Through my time under his guidance, I was able to learn Python, shell scripting, as well as how dynamic programming and algorithms play into computational biology, namely haplotype assembly.
