Aeroponic Farm Sensing Unit

Jan 2025 - May 2025

Project Details:

Designing a cost-effective, user-interfacing multi-sensor unit to continuously monitor pH, EC, temperature, and water depth in vertical aeroponic farms. Senior Capstone Project.

 

My Experience:

For our senior capstone project, our team worked with Sweetwater Urban Farms to devise a sensing unit to be used in vertical farms. As electrical lead, I was in charge of designing the PCB that sits inside the unit. This entailed researching which sensors to use and reverse engineering the circuitry within each sensor, ensuring success and stability as each signal read into our processor. Having dabbled a bit in PCB design during Junior Capstone, I was confident in the beginning that this would be a rather simple process. However, I was gravely mistaken. I did not account for the insane levels of complexity that signal conditioning circuitry presents, let alone combining all of that together in such a short timeframe and ensuring minimal interference, impedance, and voltage sag. While it required endless nights of revising schematics, testing in the lab, and breadboardng every little part, we were ultimately successful in creating a device that can save farmers up to 80+ labor hours/day, reducing labor costs alone by $750,000, annually. Through persistence and iterative design, along with successful design and integration of the packaging and software/front-end subsystems, we were able to present a fully functioning device at the Capstone Expo. Special thanks to Drew Keller for his help.

 

Skills I Learned:

- PCB Design and Fabrication

- Circuit Design

- EAGLE CAD Software

- Analog Signal Processing and Integrity Ensurance

- Reverse Engineering Analog Sensors

- Through-Hole and Surface-Mount Soldering

When I completed the course, I felt intensely proud of how far I had come. I could, even now, go for hours and hours about how each lab drove me crazy.
